# Custom orders

Custom orders are one-off special orders: a made-to-measure suit, a special-order jacket, anything you buy for a single customer. You start from the supplier's confirmation, review the details, send the customer a deposit, and receive the item when it lands.

<div class="callout callout-info">
<div class="callout-title">Last verified against the app: 2026-06-29</div>
This screen is labelled <strong>Custom orders</strong> in the app (it used to be called MTM). If a button name here doesn't match, the app changed and this page is stale — tell the team.
</div>

## Quick path

<ol class="steps">
  <li><strong>New custom order</strong> — drop in the supplier confirmation, or enter it by hand.</li>
  <li><strong>Review</strong> — check the customer, the item details, and the price.</li>
  <li><strong>Create draft</strong> — sends the customer a Shopify draft order for the deposit.</li>
  <li><strong>Mark received</strong> when it arrives, and enter the final cost.</li>
  <li><strong>Fit the customer</strong> when they come in.</li>
</ol>

## Start a custom order

From the sidebar, open <strong>Custom orders</strong> and click <strong>New custom order</strong>. Two ways in:

- <strong>Drop in the supplier confirmation</strong> — a PDF, image, or text file (up to 25 MB). The AI reads it and pulls out the customer, the supplier and reference, the items and options, the price, and any measurements it can see. This is the fast path when the brand sent you a confirmation.
- <strong>Enter manually</strong> — click this for a phoned-in order. It skips the upload and gives you a blank order to fill in.

Either way you land on the <strong>Review</strong> screen with whatever was found pre-filled.

## The Review / Sent / Fit tabs

Three tabs track where the order is:

- <strong>02 Review</strong> — check and complete the details
- <strong>03 Sent</strong> — sent to the supplier, waiting for the item
- <strong>04 Fit</strong> — fit notes after the customer picks up

(Upload is step 01 and happens before the tabs.)

## 02 Review — check the details

Everything on this screen <strong>saves on its own</strong> as you go (no Save button). Work top to bottom:

- <strong>Customer</strong> — type a name, or search your Shopify customers and link the right one. If they're new, create them here. If they've ordered before, you'll see their past custom-order count.
- <strong>Supplier</strong> — pick the brand and enter the supplier's reference number. Add an estimated delivery date.
- <strong>Measurements</strong> — toggle <strong>IN</strong> or <strong>CM</strong> at the top. Only the measurements that matter for the item type show. If the customer is linked and has been measured before, their <strong>fit profile</strong> appears on the right so you can compare.
- <strong>Items</strong> — one card per garment or product. Pick the type, fabric, and options (lapel, vents, lining, and so on), enter the unit cost and the retail price, and note any adjustments. Click <strong>Add garment</strong> for more. Custom orders aren't limited to tailoring — accessories, ties, and scarves work too.
- <strong>Pricing</strong> — set the currency, and an optional <strong>deposit</strong> in your store currency.

<div class="callout callout-tip">
<div class="callout-title">The fit profile saves you re-measuring</div>
When a customer is linked, ThreadCloud snapshots their measurements onto the order. Next time, their profile pre-fills, and over time it learns their adjustments (e.g. always shorten the sleeve a touch).
</div>

## Create the draft (take the deposit)

When the details are right, click <strong>Create draft</strong>. ThreadCloud creates a <strong>Shopify draft order</strong> for the customer with the deposit amount, and moves the order to <strong>Sent</strong>. Send the draft-order link to the customer for payment.

<div class="callout callout-warn">
<div class="callout-title">Always take the deposit</div>
Custom orders are non-returnable. The deposit is the customer's commitment before you commit to the supplier. Walk the customer through the spec before you create the draft — once it's with the supplier, changes are expensive or impossible.
</div>

## Receive on arrival

When the item lands, open the order (it's on the <strong>Sent</strong> tab) and click <strong>Mark received</strong>. Enter the final cost per item and any receiving notes, then save. You can print the receiving document (units and cost per item) for your records. Use <strong>Edit receipt</strong> if you need to correct the cost later.

## Fit notes

The <strong>Fit</strong> tab is where post-pickup fit notes will live — observed alterations that feed back into the customer's fit profile.

<div class="callout callout-info">
<div class="callout-title">Not live yet</div>
Fit notes are still being built. For now, keep alteration notes on the order's receiving notes or the customer's record, and we'll migrate them when the Fit tab ships.
</div>

## If it goes wrong

- <strong>The upload didn't read the confirmation well</strong> — that's fine. Fix the fields on Review, or start over with <strong>Enter manually</strong>.
- <strong>Create draft is greyed out</strong> — every item needs a type set. Fill in any blank item type.
- <strong>Anything else</strong> — see <a href="/runbook/">If something breaks</a>.
